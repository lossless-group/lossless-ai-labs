## Why Care?

The splash is the tree's public face — the place someone outside the team lands to see what ai-labs contains. Its rollup was frozen at four children (context-vigilance-kit, memopop-ai, dididecks-ai, augment-it) while the tree quietly grew: `corpora-builder` and `id-didi-sh` mounted as new children, `dididecks-ai` grew a `client-sites/` family of five deck workspaces, and `studies/` accumulated five sub-studies of pinned prior art. None of that was visible. Today it all is — which matters right now because the tree is being opened up to an outside collaborator.

## What's New?

- **Three new top-level children in the rollup**: `corpora-builder`, `id-didi-sh`, and `studies`.
- **Container-aware sub-child discovery** — `rollup-sync.ts` previously only auto-discovered sub-projects under a child's `apps/`. Each child can now declare `containers`: `dididecks-ai` scans both `apps/` and `client-sites/` (picking up calmstorm, chroma, humain-vc, lossless, and reach-edu decks), and `studies` scans its own direct children (`'.'`) — surfacing agent-harnesses, conversational-ui-and-native-shells, memory-layers-for-agents, open-specs-and-standards, and vector-databases. Anything with a `changelog/` or `context-v/` becomes a sub-child with full provenance.
- **Full re-sync committed** — `splash/src/rollup/` now carries 268 changelog files and 525 context-v files from 20 sources.
- **Submodule pointers advanced** to each child's pushed tip (augment-it, context-vigilance-kit, corpora-builder, and two studies), including context-vigilance-kit's corpus refresh that landed earlier today.
