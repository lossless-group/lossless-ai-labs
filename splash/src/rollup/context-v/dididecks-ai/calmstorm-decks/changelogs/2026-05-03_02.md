## Why Care?

The third scroll deck arrived clean — registry-driven propagation meant the
header pill, the bottom-right `DeckNav`, the keyboard handler, and the
index-page CTA all picked up `/thesis/version-3` without template touches.
But the pill's inner counter line started **wrapping** the moment a third
variant was registered: `V1 · BASELINE` and `(1/3)` stacked onto two lines,
and the otherwise thin header gained a centimeter of vertical weight that
broke the otherwise calm chrome.

This is the kind of small visual regression that doesn't show up until the
catalog actually grows past two. It's also the kind of thing that, left
alone, would compound — by the time the menu carries five or six variants,
the chrome would be visibly bloated, and the fix would feel like
infrastructure work instead of polish. The principle worth naming:
**navigation surfaces have to be designed for the size of the catalog they
are about to have, not the size they have today.** Today's note locks in
that posture for the variant pill so the next several decks slot in cleanly.

## What Was Built

A two-attribute fix to the inner counter span in
`src/components/basics/DeckHeader.astro`:

- `whitespace-nowrap` keeps the label and the counter on a single line so
  the pill grows horizontally instead of vertically.
- A small amount of horizontal padding (`px-2`) and a slightly wider gutter
  before the counter (`ml-1.5`) gives the longer text room to breathe inside
  the pill border.

That's it. No structural change to the markup, no new component, no new
prop. The single-line invariant is now expressed in the chrome's own CSS
rather than implicitly relying on the label staying short.

## Files Touched

```
sites/calmstorm-decks/
└── src/
    └── components/
        └── basics/
            └── DeckHeader.astro      (whitespace-nowrap + small padding tweaks on the counter span)
```

Build state: 36 pages built. Behavior unchanged on every page that does not
render the variant pill; on pages that do, the pill is now a single
horizontal strip regardless of how many decks the registry holds.

## Open Items

- **Header center-column density at five or six variants.** The pill now
  scales horizontally, which means it begins to consume real width in the
  middle of the header. At four or five variants it'll still fit
  comfortably; beyond that, a compact mode (just `(N/M)` with the label
  hidden, or a popover-style menu) is the natural next step. Trivial to
  add when it earns its place; deliberately deferred until then.
- **Per-page meta vs. registry label.** Each thesis page still passes a
  per-page `<DeckHeader meta="…" />` value alongside the registry's `label`.
  These should converge so the per-page meta defaults to derived-from-
  registry. Flagged on the previous changelog; still open.
