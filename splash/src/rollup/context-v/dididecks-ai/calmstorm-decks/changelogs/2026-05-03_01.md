## Why Care?

Two scroll decks established the *practice* of treating full-deck variants as a
first-class deliverable. Three scroll decks establish the *menu*. The
difference matters: with two options, a client is choosing between an A and a
B. With three, the conversation shifts to "which voice fits this audience" —
an editorial choice rather than a binary preference.

The third deck also confirms what the previous two suggested: **the marginal
cost of a new full-deck composition has collapsed to roughly one session of
work**. Same narratives, same theme tokens, same data files, same
`PageAsDeckWrapper`. What changes is the layout vocabulary inside seventeen
section components. Nothing in the registry, the navigation chrome, the
gate, or the index-page CTA needed manual touching beyond a single line
addition — variant 3 propagated through every surface automatically.

For a fund preparing materials for a small but heterogeneous audience —
LPs with different reading habits, partners with different design instincts,
prospects with different industries of origin — having a real menu of
compositional voices is the actual product. Variant 3 gives that menu its
third entry, and the production discipline that made it cheap is what makes
adding a fourth, fifth, or sixth equally tractable when the moment calls.

## What Was Built

### The editorial voice

Variant 3 adopts a single coherent design direction: **the printed editorial
quarterly**. Every spread carries a plate mark across the top with section
labels and a `SPREAD nn / 17` running mark, every spread closes with a
`MMXXVI · Confidential` plate at the foot, and the body sits inside hairline
rules with the typographic restraint of a print magazine. The vocabulary —
drop caps, marginal sidenotes, leader-dotted directories, italic bylines,
roman-numeral series markers, dotted leader lines, colophon footers — is
deployed consistently across all seventeen sections so the deck reads as a
single artifact rather than seventeen unrelated experiments.

A small set of utility classes (`v3-stage`, `v3-eyebrow`, `v3-section-title`,
`v3-rule`, `v3-plate`, `v3-warm`) is defined once in the slide 02 module's
`is:global` style block and reused across every subsequent slide, which keeps
the visual rhythm tight without duplicating the chrome in every file.

Color discipline stays narrow: primary blue and surface-warm cream do most of
the work, with a single state color (state-error red on the Problem masthead,
state-success green on the Opportunity counterpoint) reserved for the two
slides where editorial framing benefits from one accent.

### Registry-driven propagation

A single line added to `src/lib/scroll-decks.ts` is the only change required
to make variant 3 a first-class member of the menu:

```ts
{ href: "/thesis/version-3", label: "v3 · editorial", variantNumber: 3 },
```

From that one line:
- The header pill that appears on any scroll-deck page now reads
  `(1 of 3)` / `(2 of 3)` / `(3 of 3)` and cycles through all three.
- The bottom-right `DeckNav` does the same.
- The index-page CTA aside (refactored in this commit to map over
  `SCROLL_DECKS` instead of hardcoding two list items) renders v3
  automatically.
- The keyboard ← / → navigation between scroll decks works without changes.

This is exactly the pattern the registry was set up to enable when the
navigation chrome was built for v2. Today's commit is the first time it was
exercised, and it cost zero template touches beyond the registry line.

### Index-page CTA refactor

The "Or scroll the assembled deck" aside on `/` previously hardcoded both
deck links as inline `<li>` blocks. It now maps over the `SCROLL_DECKS`
registry. A small `deckBlurbs` lookup in the page frontmatter holds the
human-readable one-liner per deck path, so adding a fourth scroll deck is a
single line in `scroll-decks.ts` plus an optional matching blurb entry.

## Open Items

- **Header pill density.** With three variants the pill counter still fits
  on a single line. At four or five it will start to crowd the center
  column of the header at narrower viewports. Trivial to address when it
  becomes visible — just collapse the label-with-counter format into a
  shorter `(N/M)` glyph below a breakpoint.
- **Variant labels live in two places.** `scroll-decks.ts` carries a
  `label` field for chrome; each thesis page also passes a per-page
  `<DeckHeader meta="…" />` value. Worth unifying when the next variant is
  added — the per-page meta should default to derived-from-registry.
- **No content delta across v1, v2, v3.** All three render the exact same
  source data verbatim. Forking narrative copy per variant is a non-trivial
  decision (overlay system? branched narratives?) — flagged for when a
  client actually wants different copy per audience.
