## Why Care?

Walk into `src/components/basics/` today and you'll find `DeckHeader`, `DeckNav`, `MetaTags`, `GateScript` ‚Äî universal chrome, the actual basics ‚Äî sitting next to `AssetsDataPanel` and `SlidesStatusListTable`, which are feature surfaces for the audit dashboard that just got dropped into `basics/` because no other directory existed. That's how `basics/` rots: every time something new lands without an obvious home, it goes there, and eventually "basics" means "everything" which means nothing.

Underneath, the slide variants in `src/slides/by-title/` and the three teaser-section copies in `src/layouts/sections/teaser*/` redeclare the same scoped CSS for an eyebrow, a section title, a card, a chip in ~21,000 lines across 102 files. The cost of changing the card style is editing 102 places. The cost of having an opinion about the card is sustaining it in 102 places. So we don't have an opinion about the card.

And there was no surface ‚Äî for a stakeholder iterating on a single component, or for the AI agent that's supposed to compose new slides from named components ‚Äî where the available kit could be browsed. The kit exists; nobody can see it.

Today fixes the seeing part. The migration is still ahead.

## What's New?

### The plan-of-record lives in the parent, not here

`context-v/plans/Componentize-Slides-and-Establish-Component-Library.md` is in the **dididecks-ai parent**, not in this client-site, because what comes out of this work is meant to seed every future client-site we build under DidiDecks. The plan went through three drafts in a single session:

- **v0.0.1** ‚Äî the taxonomy fix and the slide-by-slide cadence. Nine directories under `src/components/`, with a decision rule for each. "‚â•2 uses to promote" as the discipline that prevents `patterns/` from rotting the way `basics/` did.
- **v0.0.2** ‚Äî the privacy-paradigm constraint. Three distribution tiers (`private` / `shared` / `public`) gate which "internet convention" components are legal in the dist. Two new component directories ‚Äî `share/` (OG, Twitter Card) and `publish/` (sitemap, llms.txt, JSON-LD, canonical) ‚Äî are physical homes for the gating rule. A new Phase 0.5 audits emissions and builds a tier resolver.
- **v0.0.3** ‚Äî the two-route split with agent-readable manifests. `/design-system/` is the contract; `/component-library/` is the catalog. Both are agent-readable via a registry + future `llms.txt` projections. We deliberately diverge from the astro-knots single-hub precedent because component-iteration is the DidiDecks product loop.

Phases 0 through 4. The plan is detailed enough to execute against, not so detailed that it commits to decisions the slide work will surface.

### Two new routes, both navigable today

- **`/design-system/`** ‚Äî the contract. Renders the two-tier color system (25 named palette swatches ‚Üí 24 semantic roles), typography, radius, elevation. The page is the live rendering of `DESIGN.md`; when tokens drift, `DESIGN.md` is updated first and the route re-renders.
- **`/component-library/`** ‚Äî the catalog hub. Nine category cards (basics, primitives, patterns, slide infrastructure, audit, share, publish, diagrams, markdown), each tagged with state (`built` / `planned` / `future`) and the decision rule that governs the category.

Both routes are `private` tier with explicit `<meta name="robots" content="noindex,nofollow">` and live behind the existing cover-page gate.

### Nine category pages + eleven inspector pages

Every card on `/component-library` clicks through to a category page; every built component on a category page clicks through to its inspector. That's:

- 9 category pages at `/component-library/[category]` ‚Äî dynamic-routed from a single template
- 11 inspector pages at `/component-library/[category]/[component]` ‚Äî dynamic-routed for the components that actually exist on disk today (4 in basics, 1 in slides, 2 in audit, 4 in markdown)
- Planned and future components render as ghost cards with reduced opacity and no link ‚Äî they're roadmap markers, visible but not navigable

The inspector pages render the five-section contract from the plan: live demo, props, CSS contract, composes-from, used-by. The demo frame is intentionally a placeholder ‚Äî per-component demos get hand-authored when the component is iterated on (Phase 2 of the plan). Everything else is real data pulled from the registry.

### `src/lib/component-registry.ts` ‚Äî the single source of truth

The hub, category pages, and inspector pages all read from the same TypeScript module. Adding a new built component is one entry in the registry; the sub-page generates automatically. This is the same registry the plan named for agent-readability ‚Äî when the embedded chat needs to compose a slide, it consumes the registry (or, eventually, its `/component-library/llms.txt` projection) to learn what's composable.

Today's registry has 31 component entries across the 9 categories ‚Äî 11 `built`, 18 `planned`, 6 `future` ‚Äî with one-line purposes, file paths for the built ones, and slots for `props`, `cssContract`, `usedBy`, and `composesFrom` that fill in as Phase 2 progresses.

### DeckHeader gained two items

The header was Table of Contents ¬∑ Scroll ¬∑ Changelog. It's now Table of Contents ¬∑ Scroll ¬∑ Changelog ¬∑ Design System ¬∑ Components. Active-state highlighting wired for both new items. The decision to put them in the header is a deliberate divergence from the astro-knots family precedent ‚Äî those sites tuck the design system into a footer link because it's contributor-facing and they treat their headers as marketing surfaces. We put it in the header because component iteration is the daily work here, not a marketing concern.

It's a known crowding problem ‚Äî five items plus the variant cycler on scroll-deck routes is approaching the budget. When the deck eventually promotes to `shared` or `public` tier the two contributor items should hide via the tier resolver. Flagged for later, not blocking now.

## The Three Iterations of the Taxonomy

The first draft of `src/components/` taxonomy was four directories. The third was nine. Each addition came from a specific failure mode the previous version exposed.

### Draft 1 ‚Äî the four-bucket cascade

```
basics/ ¬∑ primitives/ ¬∑ patterns/ ¬∑ slides/   (+ diagrams/, future)
```

The decision rule was a five-step cascade: every-page ‚Üí state-not-content ‚Üí slide-shell ‚Üí atomic ‚Üí composed-and-reused. Audit surfaces (`AssetsDataPanel`, `SlidesStatusListTable`) got their own peer `audit/` because they don't fit `basics/` ("every page") and don't fit `primitives/` ("no layout opinion") and don't fit `patterns/` ("composed for ‚â•2 slides"). That was the genesis of the "if you can't place it, the taxonomy is wrong, not your component" discipline.

### Draft 2 ‚Äî the privacy-paradigm wedge

The parent spec `Dididecks-AI-Slide-Decks-as-Code.md` commits to three distribution tiers (NS-1: private workspace ‚Üî publish surface; ¬ß"Individual ‚Üî Team": individual / team / firm-public). Some "internet convention" components ‚Äî OG tags, Twitter Card, sitemap entries, `<link rel="canonical">`, JSON-LD, third-party analytics ‚Äî are claims about the URL's audience. Rendering them at the wrong tier is a real privacy leak: an unfurl bot fetching an OG image announces the URL to whatever service the bot reports to.

So the rule-of-thumb cascade gained two leading steps:

1. **Does its presence in the dist make a publicness claim?** ‚Üí `publish/`
2. **Does it support unfurling to a known correspondent?** ‚Üí `share/`

Before any of the existing taxonomy questions get asked. `share/` and `publish/` aren't just where the components live ‚Äî the directory placement *is* the gate. The tier resolver decides whether to import from those directories per route. Components in `basics/` never need a tier check; components in `share/` and `publish/` always do.

### Draft 3 ‚Äî `/design-system/` and `/component-library/` separate

The astro-knots family (`dark-matter`, `fullstack-vc`, `banner-site`) collapses tokens and components into one `/design-system/` hub. We split:

| Route | Job | Stability |
|---|---|---|
| `/design-system/` | **The contract.** Tokens, typography, modes. Live rendering of `DESIGN.md`. | Stable. Changes ripple. |
| `/component-library/` | **The catalog.** Hub ‚Üí category ‚Üí per-component inspector. | Iterative. Per-engagement. |

`/design-system/` answers *what are the rules?* ‚Äî `/component-library/` answers *what's in the kit?* For a site whose product loop is "AI composes new slides from named components," conflating those two questions in one surface is friction. The split is the architecture.

## The Registry Is Where The Agent Reads First

The plan commits this stack: `DESIGN.md` is the canonical contract, `src/components/registry.ts` is the canonical component index, `/component-library/llms.txt` + `llms-full.txt` are the build-time projections of the registry for agent consumption. The first two exist today; the build-time `llms.txt` emission is Phase 4.

```mermaid
flowchart LR
  DM[DESIGN.md<br/>at repo root] --> DS[/design-system<br/>live rendering]
  REG[src/lib/<br/>component-registry.ts] --> CL[/component-library<br/>hub]
  REG --> CAT[/component-library/&lbrack;category&rbrack;<br/>9 pages, 1 template]
  REG --> INS[/component-library/&lbrack;cat&rbrack;/&lbrack;comp&rbrack;<br/>11 pages, 1 template]
  REG -.->|Phase 4| LLM[/component-library/llms.txt<br/>+ llms-full.txt<br/>for agents]
  style REG fill:#fef3c7,stroke:#b45309
  style DM fill:#dbeafe,stroke:#1e40af
```

The discipline: every new component lands its registry entry AND its inspector page in the same commit that introduces the component. No drift. The inspector page is generated, so the cost of the discipline is one TypeScript object literal per component, not a hand-authored Astro file per component.

That cost is small enough that it has a chance of being sustained. That's the actual reason for the registry ‚Äî not "agents want it" (though they do) but "the cost of *not* documenting a component has to be lower than the cost of documenting it, or it won't happen."

## The Audit Dashboard Tile Was The Other Option

The original plan put `/design-system/` and `/component-library/` discovery inside the audit dashboard at `/` ‚Äî as two new tiles in `AssetsDataPanel`, alongside `Companies` / `People` / `Slide Slots` / `Design Variants`. That mirrors the astro-knots footer-link discipline and keeps the deck's presentation header (Table of Contents ¬∑ Scroll ¬∑ Changelog) clean for the LP walking through the slides.

In practice, when iterating on a specific component, you want one click from anywhere in the deck ‚Äî not "go back to root, find the tile, click in." So the header won. The audit-dashboard tile pattern is still the right call when the deck promotes to a real audience tier; the tier resolver will hide the header items for `shared` and `public` decks while keeping the dashboard tiles available to contributors.

A small reversal of a confident position. Worth documenting so the same conversation doesn't replay in three months.

## What's Next?

The skeleton works. The migration is the work.

- **Phase 0 ‚Äî the relocation commit.** Move `AssetsDataPanel.astro` and `SlidesStatusListTable.astro` from `basics/` to `audit/`. Create empty `share/`, `publish/`, `primitives/`, `patterns/`, `diagrams/`. Update the four registry entries that name `basics/` paths for those files. One pure-relocation commit, no content changes.
- **Phase 0.5 ‚Äî the tier resolver + tier-aware `MetaTags`.** Build `src/lib/distribution-tier.ts` (default-private, fail-closed). Refactor `MetaTags.astro` to emit `noindex,nofollow` at `private`/`shared`. Audit existing emissions across pages. Single commit.
- **Phase 1 ‚Äî seed `primitives/`.** Five components ‚Äî `Eyebrow`, `SectionTitle`, `Subtitle`, `Chip`, `RevealItem`. Each wraps an existing utility class from `theme.css`. Registry entries land with each. No slide changes yet ‚Äî slides start consuming primitives in Phase 2.
- **Phase 2 ‚Äî the slide-by-slide loop.** Slides 01 through 17 in narrative order. Each section's three by-title variants plus three teaser sources migrated together. Patterns promoted into `patterns/` when they recur. The 102-file duplication starts collapsing here. Estimated 25‚Äì40 hours total ‚Äî not single-session.
- **Phase 3 ‚Äî cleanup pauses** at slide 8 and slide 16 to merge near-duplicate patterns and demote one-slide-only patterns back to inline.
- **Phase 4 ‚Äî fill in the inspector demos** and emit the `llms.txt` projections from the registry.

The hub at `/component-library` shows the work as a ratio: 11 built / 18 planned / 6 future. Watch that ratio invert over the coming weeks. The "built" bar is what's done.
