## 29 · Tech Bio is not Biotech

- **slot:** `29`
- **slug:** `tech-bio-vs-biotech`
- **kind:** comparison table

### Headline
Tech Bio is not Biotech

### Sub
The convergence of AI, automation, and biology is creating one of **the biggest investment opportunities** of the decade.

### Table (5 axes)
| Axis | TechBio | Biotech |
|---   |---      |---      |
| **Speed & Efficiency:** AI-Driven vs. Trial-and-Error | AI-driven drug discovery compresses timelines from 5+ years to months. | Traditional high-throughput screening is slow and costly. |
| **Scalability:** Platform-Based vs. Single Asset Focus | Scalable platforms generate multiple drug candidates, creating repeatable value. | Many firms bet on a single asset, making them high-risk. |
| **Cost Reduction:** Software Margins vs. Lab-Heavy R&D | Cloud labs, AI simulations, and automation reduce R&D costs by up to 70%. | Wet-lab experiments and clinical trials require billions in capital. |
| **Capital Efficiency:** Faster Market Entry | AI-designed molecules can reach IND stage with 80% lower costs. | Traditional methods take years to de-risk before clinical trials. |
| **Investment Opportunity:** Software-Like Scale Meets Biotech Upside | Lower failure rates, broader pipelines, and recurring revenue models. | Binary outcomes and high burn rates limit venture scalability. |
