# Firm-Wide Emergent Practices — Discovery, not prescription

## What this slide says

Lossless engages a client firm by *discovering* what's already happening across its individuals and teams, then *composing* a path forward — not by prescribing a stack and rolling it out top-down. Discovery covers three dimensions for every person and team: what's already in use (often unevenly), what's causing drag, and what's wished-for but feels out of reach. Each dimension pairs with a move — surface and streamline, isolate and demo, combine and demo — and the moves compose: reducing drag is what makes the wishful thinking reachable in the first place.

## Why this slide exists

The credibility slide answered "have you done this before." This one answers "how do you do it." The job is to show the engagement is *bottom-up and emergent*, not a stack we install. That framing matters because (a) the kinds of clients we want already mistrust top-down consulting moves, and (b) the firm-wide framing distinguishes us from per-tool vendors and single-team pilots — we work with the whole organization, finding what's already useful and what's keeping people stuck.

## Content

### The promise

**Discovery with individuals and teams across the firm — emergent practices surfaced, then composed.**

### Three dimensions of discovery, three moves

For every individual and team we engage with, we explore three things and pair each with a specific kind of move:

#### 1. Current state of use

**What we find:** the tools, workarounds, and quiet wins already in play — usually unevenly distributed, often invisible to leadership.

**What we do:** **uncover the gems, surface them firm-wide, streamline them so they're durable.** The teammate who built a great prompt template for memo drafts didn't tell anyone; we find that template, polish it, make it the firm's.

#### 2. Current anxieties, stresses, overwhelm

**What we find:** the "drag" — the parts of work that drain energy without producing value. Repetitive document shuffling, dread of certain meetings, the inbox tax, the search-for-the-file tax.

**What we do:** **identify and isolate the drag, then propose solutions with live demonstrations.** Not slide-ware solutions — actual demos run against the team's real workflow.

#### 3. "Wishful thinking" / idealized work that's out of reach

**What we find:** the "if only we had time to…" — the analytical work, the relationship work, the market-research work that the team would *want* to do if the drag weren't consuming the day.

**What we do:** **combine wishful thinking with reduced drag.** Demonstrations that show the wished-for work becoming reachable *because* the drag was removed. The drag-reduction creates the headroom; the wishful-thinking work fills it.

### The compositional claim

These three dimensions are *not* independent. They compose:

> Drag reduction creates the headroom; the wishful work fills it. The current-state gems become the building blocks for both.

That compositional relationship is the differentiator. A per-tool vendor solves dimension 1. A consultancy solves dimension 2 with a process redesign. A "future of work" pitch sells dimension 3 as aspiration. Lossless does all three *and lets them feed each other*.

### Layout candidates

- **Three-column** — one column per dimension, find-mode on top, solution-mode on bottom, with a connecting band underneath showing the compositional claim (arrow from "drag" column into "wishful" column).
- **Three-row pipeline** — find on the left, do on the right, three rows stacked. The arrow shape carries the discovery → composition motion naturally.
- **Spiral / funnel** — dimensions feeding inward to a centerpoint where the composed engagement plan lives. Risk: too "consultant-deck" if the metaphor is hit too hard.
- **Two-pane: top is the three dimensions, bottom is one big demo screenshot or short loop** — the demo IS the methodology evidence. Strongest for an in-person partner conversation; risky for skim-readers.

## Substantiation

- **Calmstorm** — discovery of an existing dealflow workflow that lived in spreadsheets + a fundraise process that lived in people's heads; surface-and-streamline followed by drag-reduction in inbox triage and memo drafts
- **Chroma** — discovery of investor-credibility communication that was happening ad-hoc; surfaced into the credibility-card pattern (multi-investor layout in `client-sites/chroma-decks`)
- **Reach** — discovery of fundraising surface needs at an education-sector engagement; education-sector precedent for any future Podium-shaped work
- **Humain** — flat operating-company engagement; discovery surfaced the gap between investor-anchored and operator-anchored credibility framing
- **Pattern in common** — every engagement above started with "what's already happening here" before "what should we add"

For the DD-grade version of this slide, want at least one concrete *gem-surfacing* quote per engagement (a teammate's quiet win that became firm-wide).

## Authoring notes

- **The compositional claim is the differentiator.** Easy to write the three dimensions as a bullet list and lose it. Whatever layout we pick has to make the *composition* visible — drag-reduction enabling wishful-thinking has to be a thing the slide *shows*, not a thing the speaker has to add verbally.
- **"Emergent" as a load-bearing word.** It's doing two jobs: (a) emergent across the firm — practices show up unevenly and need to be surfaced, (b) emergent over time — the engagement plan emerges from discovery rather than being prescribed. Worth checking that both meanings survive the visual treatment.
- **The "drag" framing is honest but charged.** Engineering and PE-adjacent audiences will respond; partner-track culture sometimes resists naming workplace stress. Watch the audience read in the deck variant.
- **Demonstrations are the proof.** The word "demonstrate" appears in the move-text for two of the three dimensions and is the credible bit — without live-demo evidence, this slide reads as positioning rather than methodology. May want a companion slide later showing a specific demo (and may want a video / interactive embed in the play-UI variant).
- **"Firm-wide" scope.** Important the slide doesn't promise *every individual in the firm* — that would over-scope. The promise is firm-wide *engagement* (we don't ignore the long tail; we surface what's distributed), not firm-wide *coverage* of every person in equal depth. Wording matters.
- **Order in the arc.** Almost certainly *after* credibility, almost certainly *before* the capability / specific-outputs slides. May be the strongest slide in the deck and want a moment to itself rather than competing with adjacent slides for attention.

## Open questions

- Does this slide need a companion slide showing one specific drag-reduction → wishful-thinking story end-to-end (a "before / what we did / after" mini case)? The compositional claim might land harder if it's instantiated once, even briefly.
- Is "Discovery, not prescription" the right subtitle, or does it sound consultant-y in a way that undercuts the rest of the deck's voice? Alternatives: "Surface what's already working. Reduce what's draining. Make the wished-for reachable." (longer, but louder), or "Emergent, not installed." (tighter).
- Should the three dimensions get equal visual weight, or should "drag reduction" carry slightly more (since it's the *enabler* of dimension 3 per the compositional claim)?
