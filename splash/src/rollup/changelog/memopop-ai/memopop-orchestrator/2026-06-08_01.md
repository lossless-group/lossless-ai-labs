# Changelog — 2026-06-08_01

## What this entry is

A two-line structural change at the orchestrator level: register a new
firm-private submodule, pin its initial commit. The submodule path is
`io/alpha-jwc`; the upstream is `lossless-group/alpha-jwc-secure`. It
matches the pattern every other firm-private submodule under `io/`
follows (`alpha-partners`, `hypernova`, `avalanche`, `dark-matter`,
`emerge`, `humain`). Alpha JWC is now the sixth firm wired in this way.

## What this entry deliberately is NOT

A description of the firm's brand config, its customized outline, the
deals already set up against it, the deck-input handling, the codified
`Sources.md`, or any memo content. All of that lives in the
`alpha-jwc-secure` private repo and is documented in that submodule's
own `changelog/` — not here. The CLAUDE.md scope rule introduced in
the next entry makes this explicit; this entry is the first one
written in deliberate respect of it.

## What the pattern looks like

The full procedure (brand acquisition, firm-directory scaffolding,
outline selection, first deal setup, optional private-repo split) is
documented as the `setup-new-firm` agent-skill that landed in the
`2026-06-14_01` entry. The next firm onboarded should follow that
skill rather than improvising — Alpha JWC's onboarding surfaced
enough failure modes (primary-color heuristic wrong, deck analyst
misreading DocSend watermarks as the company name, firm-geography
leaking into company-specific risk prose, citation enrichment
fabricating URLs even in codified mode) that consolidating the
procedure was overdue.

## What's next

- Alpha JWC's first deal (Panthalassa Series B) is in motion inside
  the submodule; bumps to `io/alpha-jwc` advance the pinned sha as
  the firm's work lands, but stay as quiet `bump(submodule):` entries
  per the CLAUDE.md rule.
- The `setup-new-firm` skill (see `2026-06-14_01`) is the reusable
  output of this onboarding.
